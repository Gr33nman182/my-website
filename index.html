How to Connect to Your Media Servers 🎬

Why the Change?

Plex has started charging for remote streaming, meaning watching outside of my house is no longer free.

To keep things simple and cost-free, I recommend using Jellyfin, a completely free alternative with a similar interface.

If you still prefer Plex, no problem! We have a workaround using Tailscale, which tricks Plex into thinking you're at my house, restoring free streaming. It may slightly affect streaming performance.

Connecting to Jellyfin (Recommended)

  • 🧭Step 1: Open a web browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, etc.)
  • 🌐Step 2: Visit tmulligan.duckdns.org
  • 🔐Step 3: Log in using the username (ask me if you don't know it) and password: changeme
  • 🔑Step 4: Go to Settings → Profile and change your password
  • 🧰Step 5: If Jellyfin is down, use jelly4k.duckdns.org (same credentials)
  • 🎉Step 6: Enjoy watching!

Requesting Movies & Shows on Jellyfin

  • 🌍Step 1: Open a web browser
  • 📝Step 2: Visit finrequest.duckdns.org
  • 🔐Step 3: Log in with your Jellyfin username/password
  • 🔍Step 4: Search for the title and click Request to add it to the library
Note: The request page might take a minute to load—please be patient!

Connecting to Plex (via Tailscale)

  • ⬇️Step 1: Download and install Tailscale for your device
  • 🔐Step 2: Open Tailscale and contact me for login details
  • ✅Step 3: Ensure Tailscale is running (look for the tray icon)

Option A: Use the Plex App (Recommended)

  • Open the Plex app
  • Add a manual server: plexnas.stoat-bull.ts.net:32400
  • Log in with your Plex credentials

Option B: Use a Web Browser

  • Ensure Tailscale is running
  • Visit http://plexnas.stoat-bull.ts.net:32400 (use http, not https)
  • Log in with your Plex username/password
